Welcome to Gutenkraft


The Problem.

According to the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), this is the level of foreign aid as of year 2020 (The year of COVID-19 pandemic)was $161.2 billion. This was an increase of about 3.5% from 2019 levels and the highest level yet.
Inspite of this increase in foreign aid generosity, questions about the its effectiveness of foreign aid continues to be asked because of the persistence of problems into which much fund had be sunk in the past. This may be driving some developed nations to cut-back on the level of aid they are willing to provide in the future. The challenge of ensuring foreign aid effectiveness is in large part due to its use mosty for implementing band-aid sort of solutions, which are often unsustainable quick fixes .

Our Position.

At Gutenkraft, we believe the lack of appropriate technological and other base growth infrastructures (both at individual and communal levels) is a key factor responsible for driving these unsustainable quick fix approaches to foreign aid deployment.

Who We Are.

Driven by the desire to make foreign aid effective as well as a tool to enable self-sufficiency of developing nations, Gutenkraft Labs is set-up as a non-profit technology research and development (R&D) outfit focused exclusively on designing, developing and deploying building blocks of necessary technological infrastructures needed to drive growth and improve quality of life in developing nations. This focus is codified in Gutenkraft's mission and vision.

Our Priority.

Gutenkraft's Mission:

To innovate, improve and adapt public domain technologies to solve problems in developing nations.

Gutenkraft's Vision:

To build affordable and sustainable world class technology solutions in large scale.
