Embedded Ops

The embedded ops focuses the development of electronic solutions by the application of custom or off-the-shelf hardware such as

  • Electronic prototyping boards and single board or system on chip (SoC) computers,
  • Application specific integrated chips (ASICs) boards and
  • Field programmable gate array (FPGA) boards

as well as real-time operating systems (RTOS) softwares such as

  • Embedded Linux,
  • FreeRTOS
  • Zephyr and,
  • any other promising RTOS

and embedded system application software programming tools such as

  • Embedded C
  • Rust language
  • Qt
  • Python language? (Yes that Python)
    • and
  • Hardware description languages (HDL) such as Verilog and VHDL

Embedded systems are crucial to ensuring smooth human day to day operations. From those in microwave ovens, washing machines to those in cars, industial machines and avionics in aeroplanes. Embedded systems are almost always present and their ubiquity is about to be turbo-charged by mass adoption of the Internet of things (IoTs) at the heart of smart systems (such as smart cities and smart electricity grids) as wells as in Industry 4.0 applications supported by super mobile networks from 5G and beyond.

The quick response time and clean nature of electronic systems over mechnical systems has given them an overwhelming command of domestic systems as well as a significant hold (along side pneumatic systems) in industrial controls systems. Embedded systems (which are electronic at heart) are important for the development of electronics based solutions.

The embedded ops unit's objective is to develop and muster embedded systems skills to serve the goal of a Gutekraft project where needed.
